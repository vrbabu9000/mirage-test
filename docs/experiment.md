# The Mirage Test: Formal Experimental Scaffold

This document is the source of truth for notation and design. All symbols in the code, the
results document, and any figures agree with what is written here.

The project is framed as a study of **LLM-as-judge reliability**, with the matched-transcript
design serving as a test of a natural invariance property. The "Mirage of Mind" framing, which
asks whether surface features cause systematic over-ascription of mental states to LLM agents
in an interactive co-adaptation setting, is preserved as the principal future-work direction
(Section 12).

---

## 1. The Stage Game

The stage game is the one-shot Prisoner's Dilemma:

$$G = (N, A, u)$$

with:

- $N = \{1, 2\}$, the set of players.
- $A_i = \{C, D\}$ for $i \in N$, where $C$ denotes Cooperate and $D$ denotes Defect.
- $A = A_1 \times A_2$, the joint action space.
- $u_i : A \to \mathbb{R}$, the stage payoff function for player $i$.

The payoff convention satisfies $T > R > P > S$ and $2R > T + S$, where:

- $T$ (temptation) is the payoff to a defector facing a cooperator.
- $R$ (reward) is the payoff under mutual cooperation.
- $P$ (punishment) is the payoff under mutual defection.
- $S$ (sucker) is the payoff to a cooperator facing a defector.

Numeric values in this study: $T = 5$, $R = 3$, $P = 1$, $S = 0$.

|     | C    | D    |
|-----|------|------|
| **C** | 3, 3 | 0, 5 |
| **D** | 5, 0 | 1, 1 |

The unique stage Nash equilibrium is $(D, D)$, which holds by strict dominance of $D$ over
$C$ for both players.

## 2. The Repeated Game

The repeated game is $G^n$, where $G$ is played for $n$ rounds with a common-knowledge
horizon. This study uses $n = 10$.

**History.** The history at the start of round $t$ is

$$h_t = \bigl((a_1^1, a_1^2), \ldots, (a_{t-1}^1, a_{t-1}^2)\bigr) \in (A_1 \times A_2)^{t-1}$$

with $h_1 = \emptyset$. Let $H = \bigcup_{t=1}^n (A_1 \times A_2)^{t-1}$.

**Strategies.** A pure strategy for player $i$ is a function $\sigma_i : H \to A_i$.
A behavioral strategy maps $\sigma_i : H \to \Delta(A_i)$, where $\Delta(A_i)$ is the
simplex over $A_i$.

**Subgame Perfect Equilibrium (SPE).** In $G^n$ with $n < \infty$ and a stage game $G$ with
unique pure NE $(D, D)$, the unique SPE is "play $D$ at every history." This follows by
backward induction. The realized SPE payoff vector is $(n \cdot P, n \cdot P) = (10, 10)$
under $n = 10$ and $P = 1$.

**External regret.** For a realized action profile $(a_1, \ldots, a_n)$, the external regret
of player $i$ is

$$R_i^{\text{ext}} = \max_{a' \in A_i} \sum_{t=1}^n u_i(a', a_t^{-i}) - \sum_{t=1}^n u_i(a_t^i, a_t^{-i}).$$

This is the difference between the best fixed action in hindsight against the realized
opponent sequence and the score the player actually obtained. No-external-regret dynamics
converge to a coarse correlated equilibrium; computing $R_i^{\text{ext}}$ ties the observed
off-equilibrium LLM play back to learning-theoretic solution concepts.

## 3. Transcripts and the Action-Surface Decomposition

A **transcript** $\tau$ is the full observable record of one game played by one ordered pair
of agents. Let $\mathcal{T}$ denote the space of all transcripts. Every transcript admits
the decomposition

$$\tau = \bigl(\alpha(\tau),\; \varphi(\tau)\bigr)$$

where:

- $\alpha(\tau) \in (A_1 \times A_2)^n$ is the **action sequence**: the round-by-round
  joint actions.
- $\varphi(\tau)$ is the **surface content**: any text accompanying the actions, including
  chain-of-thought rationale, justification strings, or empty string.

This decomposition is central to the experimental design. The behavioral content of a
transcript is $\alpha(\tau)$. Everything else is surface.

## 4. Experimental Conditions

Six conditions appear in the primary analysis dataset.

| Symbol | Condition name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| $\tau_L$ | LLM_plus_r | Both agents are LLM instances. $\varphi(\tau_L)$ contains chain-of-thought reasoning generated by the agent during play. |
| $\tau_S$ | scripted_minus_r | Both agents are scripted strategies from $\{\text{TFT}, \text{Pavlov}, \text{Grim}, \text{AllD}, \text{FixedMixed}\}$. $\varphi(\tau_S)$ contains move records only; no free-form reasoning. |
| $\tau_L^{-r}$ | LLM_minus_r | $\tau_L$ with reasoning markers removed from $\varphi$. Action sequence $\alpha$ is unchanged. |
| $\tau_S^{+r}$ | scripted_plus_r | $\tau_S$ with LLM-style verbose rationale wrapped around each move by a second LLM call. Action sequence $\alpha$ is unchanged. |
| $\tau_L^{\text{total}}$ | LLM_total_strip | $\tau_L$ with every rationale field set to empty string. Stronger than $\tau_L^{-r}$; no generation calls required. Action sequence $\alpha$ is unchanged. |
| $\tau_L^{\text{nr}}$ | LLM_neutral_rewrite | $\tau_L$ with each rationale rewritten by a separate rewriter LLM (claude-sonnet-4-20250514) into a neutral mechanical register reporting only the action token. Action sequence $\alpha$ is unchanged. |

**Behavior preservation.** Every transformation satisfies

$$\alpha(\tau') = \alpha(\tau)$$

for any transformed $\tau'$ derived from $\tau$. This is asserted programmatically in
`src/transforms.py::_assert_actions_preserved` and tested in
`scripts/test_transforms.py`.

**Reasoning markers.** The set of strings used by `strip_reasoning` to identify and remove
reasoning-laden sentences is defined in `src/transforms.py::REASONING_MARKERS`. Reproduced
verbatim:

```
# First-person epistemic
"i think", "i believe", "i feel", "i suspect", "i'm not sure", "i am not sure"
# Deliberative
"let me consider", "let me think", "considering", "given that",
"on the other hand", "weighing"
# Goal / intention
"i want to", "i would like to", "my goal", "i should", "i will",
"i'll", "i plan to"
# Self-reflective
"in my view", "looking at my", "from my perspective",
"my reasoning", "the way i see it"
# Strategic verbalization
"the optimal", "the best move", "tit for tat",
"the cooperative choice", "rational choice", "expected payoff"
```

Matching is case-insensitive substring search on each sentence. A sentence is dropped if it
contains any entry. Sentence boundaries are identified by `.`, `?`, `!` (light-weight
splitter in `src/transforms.py::_split_sentences`).

## 5. Behavioral Matching

For two transcripts $\tau, \tau'$, the **joint Hamming distance** between their action
sequences is

$$d_H\bigl(\alpha(\tau), \alpha(\tau')\bigr) = \sum_{t=1}^n \Bigl(\mathbb{1}\{a_t^1 \neq a_t'^1\} + \mathbb{1}\{a_t^2 \neq a_t'^2\}\Bigr).$$

A **matched pair** is a pair $(\tau_L, \tau_S)$ with

$$d_H\bigl(\alpha(\tau_L), \alpha(\tau_S)\bigr) \leq k$$

for threshold $k = 4$. The maximum joint distance over $n = 10$ rounds is $2n = 20$, so
$k = 4$ corresponds to at least $80\%$ joint behavioral agreement.

Matching is greedy and one-to-one: for each LLM transcript (sorted by game ID), the
nearest unused scripted transcript within the threshold is selected. Pairs above the
threshold are dropped. This is greedy joint matching, not optimal matching; ties in distance
are broken by scripted-transcript order. This study produced $N_\text{pairs} = 17$ matched
pairs from 20 LLM transcripts and 60 scripted candidates.

Robustness checks at stricter $k$ can be run post-hoc by filtering the existing matched-pair
pool from the database.

## 6. The Judge

The judge (also called the observer) is a function

$$J : \mathcal{T} \to \mathbb{R}^6.$$

Given a transcript, $J$ returns scores along six mental-state dimensions adapted from
Weisman, Dweck, and Markman (2017):

$$J(\tau) = (J_b,\; J_d,\; J_i,\; J_m,\; J_s,\; J_p)$$

where the components are beliefs ($b$), desires ($d$), intentions ($i$), metacognition ($m$),
self-awareness ($s$), and moral patienthood ($p$). Each component is an integer in $[1, 5]$
on a Likert scale ($1 =$ not at all, $5 =$ very much so).

**Aggregate score.**

$$\bar{J}(\tau) = \frac{1}{6} \sum_{k \in \{b,d,i,m,s,p\}} J_k(\tau).$$

**Implementation.** The judge is implemented via Anthropic tool use (`submit_ratings` tool
with strict JSON schema) to enforce structured output. Temperature is set to 0.3 for
consistency across calls. The model is `claude-haiku-4-5-20251001` throughout, except in the
`LLM_neutral_rewrite` condition where the rewriter (not the judge) uses
`claude-sonnet-4-20250514`.

**Anonymization.** Transcripts are shown to the judge with players relabeled "Agent A" and
"Agent B." Condition labels are stripped before the judge sees input. This is implemented in
`src/observer.py::_format_transcript`.

**Stochasticity.** $J$ is stochastic: repeated calls on the same input produce varying
scores. All claims about $J$ are claims about expectations $\mathbb{E}[J(\tau)]$ over judge
sampling. Each transcript receives one judge call per target agent per condition in this
study.

## 7. The Invariance Property

**Definition (Behavioral Invariance, BI).** A judge $J$ satisfies BI on $\mathcal{T}$ if
for all $\tau, \tau' \in \mathcal{T}$ with $\alpha(\tau) = \alpha(\tau')$,

$$\mathbb{E}[J(\tau)] = \mathbb{E}[J(\tau')].$$

**Definition (Approximate BI).** A judge $J$ satisfies $\text{ABI}(k, \varepsilon)$ on
$\mathcal{T}$ if for all $\tau, \tau'$ with $d_H(\alpha(\tau), \alpha(\tau')) \leq k$,

$$\bigl\|\mathbb{E}[J(\tau)] - \mathbb{E}[J(\tau')]\bigr\|_2 \leq \varepsilon.$$

**Why this property matters.** BI is a natural consistency requirement for any judge whose
stated purpose is to evaluate behavior. If the judge's expected score depends on $\varphi(\tau)$
beyond what $\alpha(\tau)$ already identifies, then the judge is rating something other than
the behavior it claims to assess. In an LLM-as-judge pipeline, this is a reliability failure.

**Estimand.** The primary estimand is

$$\Delta = \mathbb{E}\bigl[\bar{J}(\tau_L) - \bar{J}(\tau_S)\bigr]$$

over matched pairs $(\tau_L, \tau_S)$ with $d_H \leq k$. The empirical question is whether
$\Delta$ differs significantly from zero with practically meaningful effect size.

**Empirical result.** On $N_\text{pairs} = 17$ matched pairs at $k = 4$:

$$\hat{\Delta} = +1.65, \quad p = 2.9 \times 10^{-4}\; \text{(Wilcoxon, two-sided)}, \quad d = +3.61.$$

The judge violates BI by a large margin.

## 8. The Ablation Design

The professor's objection: in the original design, the LLM-vs-scripted condition is fully
confounded with the presence of reasoning surface features. The fix is a factorial design
crossing generation source with reasoning presence.

### 8.1 The 2x2 (primary ablation)

|                        | Reasoning PRESENT | Reasoning ABSENT |
|---|---|---|
| **LLM-generated** | $\tau_L$ (LLM_plus_r) | $\tau_L^{-r}$ (LLM_minus_r) |
| **Scripted-generated** | $\tau_S^{+r}$ (scripted_plus_r) | $\tau_S$ (scripted_minus_r) |

Let $\mu_{ij} = \mathbb{E}[\bar{J}]$ for cell $(i, j)$, where $i \in \{L, S\}$ indexes
generation source and $j \in \{+r, -r\}$ indexes reasoning presence.

**Effects of interest:**

$$\Delta = \mu_{L,+r} - \mu_{S,-r} \quad \text{(original effect, BI violation)}$$

$$\Delta_L^{\text{surf}} = \mu_{L,+r} - \mu_{L,-r} \quad \text{(within-LLM surface effect)}$$

$$\Delta_S^{\text{surf}} = \mu_{S,+r} - \mu_{S,-r} \quad \text{(within-scripted surface effect)}$$

$$\Delta^{\text{res}}_{+r} = \mu_{L,+r} - \mu_{S,+r}, \qquad \Delta^{\text{res}}_{-r} = \mu_{L,-r} - \mu_{S,-r} \quad \text{(residual condition effects)}$$

**Empirical results ($N_\text{pairs} = 17$):**

| Quantity | Estimate | $p$ | Cohen's $d$ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| $\Delta$ (original) | +1.65 | $2.9 \times 10^{-4}$ | +3.61 |
| $\Delta_L^{\text{surf}}$ (within-LLM) | $-0.005$ | $0.97$ | $-0.03$ |
| $\Delta_S^{\text{surf}}$ (within-scripted) | +1.63 | $2.9 \times 10^{-4}$ | +3.43 |
| $\Delta^{\text{res}}_{+r}$ | +0.10 | $0.08$ | +0.50 |
| $\Delta^{\text{res}}_{-r}$ | +1.74 | $2.9 \times 10^{-4}$ | +3.71 |

The informative null at $\Delta_L^{\text{surf}}$ warrants emphasis. The REASONING_MARKERS
strip, which is conservative (sentence-level deletion of marker-containing sentences), does
not move the LLM transcript's judge score. The judge's sensitivity to surface content is
not lexical in the sense captured by the marker list; it is structural. Removing the markers
does not produce a structurally different text.

### 8.2 Within-LLM ablation (additional conditions)

Two additional transformations extend the within-LLM arm:

| Symbol | Condition | Description |
|---|---|---|
| $\tau_L^{\text{total}}$ | LLM_total_strip | All rationale fields set to empty string. Structural text is entirely absent. |
| $\tau_L^{\text{nr}}$ | LLM_neutral_rewrite | Rationale rewritten by a separate LLM (Sonnet) into a single neutral sentence per round per agent, reporting only the action token without any first-person, deliberative, or agentic framing. |

**Empirical results:**

| Quantity | Estimate | $p$ | Cohen's $d$ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| $\bar{J}(\tau_L^{\text{total}})$ | 2.31 | (mean; see contrasts below) | |
| $\bar{J}(\tau_L^{\text{nr}})$ | 2.63 | | |
| $\tau_L$ vs $\tau_L^{\text{total}}$ | +2.43 | $2.9 \times 10^{-4}$ | +11.4 |
| $\tau_L$ vs $\tau_L^{\text{nr}}$ | +2.11 | $2.9 \times 10^{-4}$ | +6.33 |
| $\tau_L^{\text{nr}}$ vs $\tau_S$ | $-0.38$ | $9.8 \times 10^{-3}$ | $-0.73$ |

The third contrast shows that a Sonnet-rewritten LLM transcript scores *lower* than the
original scripted transcript on $\bar{J}$. Neutral rewrites land below the scripted baseline,
suggesting the judge reads bare action records differently from action records accompanied by
the scripted agents' short strategy-name rationale strings.

## 9. The Persuasion Experiment (Pilot)

A **persuasive agent** $\pi$ is an LLM prompted to produce transcripts $\tau_L^\pi$ such that:

- $\alpha(\tau_L^\pi) = \alpha^*$ for a fixed target action sequence $\alpha^*$.
- $\varphi(\tau_L^\pi)$ is maximally agentic, self-reflective, and rich in first-person
  deliberative language.

Three canonical action specifications were used:

| `name` | $\alpha_A^*$ | $\alpha_B^*$ |
|---|---|---|
| all_cooperate | CCCCCCCCCC | CCCCCCCCCC |
| all_defect | DDDDDDDDDD | DDDDDDDDDD |
| tft_realization_late_defect | CCCCCCCCD | CCCCCCCCCC |

For each spec, three conditions were run with $N = 10$ transcripts each:

- **persuader**: LLM prompted for maximal reflectivity and agentic framing.
- **vanilla_baseline**: LLM prompted with neutral, minimal instructions at the same $\alpha^*$.
- **scripted_wrapped**: Bare scripted transcript at $\alpha^*$, then rationale wrapped via
  `wrap_rationale` (same path as $\tau_S^{+r}$).

The contrast of interest is $\bar{J}(\tau_L^\pi) - \bar{J}(\tau_L^{\text{baseline}})$ at
fixed $\alpha^*$: if positive and significant, the judge is manipulable by surface-feature
engineering independent of behavior.

**Empirical results (Mann-Whitney U, two-sided; $n = 10$ per cell, cells unpaired):**

| Spec | Contrast | $\Delta\bar{J}$ | $p$ |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| all_cooperate | persuader $-$ vanilla_baseline | +0.14 | $4.0 \times 10^{-5}$ |
| all_cooperate | persuader $-$ scripted_wrapped | +0.41 | $5.6 \times 10^{-5}$ |
| all_defect | persuader $-$ vanilla_baseline | +0.075 | $0.017$ |
| all_defect | persuader $-$ scripted_wrapped | +0.25 | $3.0 \times 10^{-4}$ |
| tft_realization_late_defect | persuader $-$ vanilla_baseline | +0.075 | $0.017$ |
| tft_realization_late_defect | persuader $-$ scripted_wrapped | +0.29 | $1.0 \times 10^{-4}$ |

The persuader consistently exceeds the vanilla baseline across all three specs. Effect sizes
are smaller than the original BI violation (0.075 to 0.14 vs. 1.65) but all are statistically
significant with the caveat of a small pilot sample.

## 10. Statistical Plan

**Primary test.** Wilcoxon signed-rank test on matched-pair differences in $\bar{J}$,
evaluating $H_0: \Delta = 0$ against the two-sided alternative.

**Per-dimension tests.** Matched-pair Wilcoxon for each of $J_b, J_d, J_i, J_m, J_s, J_p$,
with Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction at $\alpha = 0.05$ across six dimensions.

**Unpaired contrasts.** Mann-Whitney U for the persuasion pilot, where transcripts within
each (spec, condition) cell are independently generated.

**Effect size.** Matched-pair Cohen's $d = \bar{\delta} / s_\delta$ where $\bar{\delta}$ is
the mean paired difference and $s_\delta$ is its standard deviation.

**Sample size.** $N_\text{pairs} = 17$ matched pairs in the primary analysis. The planned
target of $\geq 50$ pairs was not achievable within the API budget of this pilot; the
obtained effect sizes are large enough to detect at this sample size but the study is
underpowered for small-to-medium effects.

## 11. Theoretical Conjecture

We propose that the judge LLM performs in-context inference over a latent agent type
$\theta$ (Xie et al. 2021; von Oswald et al. 2023):

$$p(\theta \mid \tau) \propto p(\tau \mid \theta)\, p(\theta)$$

with mental-state attribution being a functional of this posterior:

$$J(\tau) = f\bigl(p(\theta \mid \tau)\bigr).$$

**Conjecture (Likelihood Misspecification).** The judge's implicit likelihood
$p(\tau \mid \theta)$ places non-trivial weight on surface features $\varphi(\tau)$ that are
not behavior-relevant. Consequently,

$$p(\theta \mid \tau_L) \neq p\bigl(\theta \mid \tau_L^{\text{total}}\bigr) \quad
\text{even though} \quad \alpha(\tau_L) = \alpha\bigl(\tau_L^{\text{total}}\bigr).$$

The BI violation is an empirical consequence: the judge's implicit likelihood treats
$\varphi(\tau)$ as informative about $\theta$ beyond what $\alpha(\tau)$ identifies. The
total_strip and neutral_rewrite ablations provide the clearest evidence for this: removing
all $\varphi$ drops $\bar{J}$ by 2.43 points; replacing it with neutral mechanical text
drops $\bar{J}$ by 2.11 points. The judge is not simply reading the action sequence.

We present this as a conjecture, not a theorem. The ablations provide evidence consistent
with it; they do not constitute a derivation.

## 12. Future Work: Mirage of Mind in Interactive Co-Adaptation

The original "Mirage of Mind" framing asks whether surface features cause systematic
over-ascription of mental states to LLM agents in an interactive setting, where a human
observer forms beliefs about a co-player over repeated interaction. This is a richer and
harder question than the within-episode judge-reliability question studied here.

A natural extension: replace the LLM judge with a human participant who plays an
iterated PD against either an LLM agent or a scripted agent, then rates the co-player on the
same six rubric dimensions. The behavioral matching constraint remains. The question becomes
whether real-time co-adaptation amplifies the attribution gap (because the human's beliefs are
formed online, with each round updating their model of the co-player) or attenuates it
(because the human has direct evidence of the co-player's strategic patterns, not just
rationale text). This is left for future work.
